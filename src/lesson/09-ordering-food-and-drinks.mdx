## Vocabulary and expressions relating to Singaporean food

| **Common terms**  | **Meaning**                                                                                                            |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Makan             | To eat                                                                                                                 |
| Dabao             | Take out / to go                                                                                                       |
| Eat / Having here | For here/dine-in                                                                                                       |
| Chope             | Calling dibs on, analogous to an informal reservation                                                                  |
| Shiok             | Extremely satisfying, feel-good                                                                                        |
| Auntie / Uncle    | term of respect for an older woman / man (use with caution on younger folk who might not wished to be viewed as older) |

## How to order drinks in Singapore

### Common Kopitiam terminology

| **Common terms** | **Meaning**                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Kopi             | Coffee                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Teh              | Tea                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| O                | No milk. Usually appended behind Kopi or Teh to form "Kopi-O" or "Teh-O". The "O" is derived from a Hokkien term, which means black. Thus, this means "black coffee"                        |
| C                | Evaporated milk instead of condensed milk. No one actually knows what the "C" stands for. Some believe it stands for "Carnation", which is a popular brand of evaporated milk in Singapore. |
| Kosong           | Kosong in Malay stands for Zero. This means no sugar in this context.                                                                                                                       |
| Siew             | In hokkien, "Siew" means less or reduce. "Siew Dai" is usually used to say we want less sweetness.                                                                                          |
| Gah              | In hokkien, "Gah" means daring in Hokkien. "Gah Dai" is usually used to say we want more sweetness.                                                                                         |
| Dai              | Sweetness                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Gao              | In hokkien, "Gao" means thick. Thus, "Kopi Gao" would mean we want thick coffee, or more coffee to be added.                                                                                |
| Po               | In hokkien, "Po" means thin. "Kopi Po" would mean we wan thin coffee                                                                                                                        |
| Peng             | Ice.                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Yuan Yang        | A mixture of tea and coffee                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Tarik            | In malay, "Tarik" means pull. This refers tot he action of continuously pouring hot coffee or tea between two cups, an action we refer to as "pulling" or "Tarik".                          |

## Other drinks we can order in Singapore

| **Drink**     | **Description**                                                                                                                                  |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Milo Dinosaur | Iced Milo with Condensed milk and sugar, and a lot of milo powder sprinkled on top.                                                              |
| Milo Godzilla | Iced milo with condensed milk and sugar, with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.                                                                      |
| Diao Yu       | Chinese Tea in Tea Bag. "Diao Yu" means fishing in Mandarin Chinese. Placing the tea bag into chinese tea glass resembles the action of fishing. |
| Teh Tarik     | Hot tea that is pulled between two cups.                                                                                                         |
